Essential Vitamins for Health

Vitamins are organic compounds required in small amounts for normal body function. They play key roles in metabolism, immunity, growth, and overall health. Fat-soluble vitamins include A, D, E, K, while water-soluble vitamins include Vitamin C and the B-complex group. Proper intake is essential for preventing deficiency-related health issues.

Fat-Soluble Vitamins

These vitamins dissolve in fats and require dietary fat for absorption. They can be stored in body tissues; excess intake may lead to toxicity.

Vitamin A (Retinol / Beta-carotene)

Supports vision, healthy skin, mucous membranes, and immunity.
Sources: Carrots, spinach, pumpkins, sweet potatoes, eggs, butter, liver, fish oils.
Deficiency: Night blindness, dry skin.

Vitamin D (Calciferol)

"Sunshine vitamin" for calcium and phosphorus absorption, strong bones, immune support.
Sources: Sunlight, fortified milk, egg yolk, salmon, tuna.
Deficiency: Rickets (children), osteomalacia (adults).

Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

Acts as an antioxidant protecting cells from free radicals.
Sources: Nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, spinach, broccoli, whole grains.
Deficiency: Muscle weakness.

Vitamin K

Essential for blood clotting and bone health.
Sources: Spinach, kale, broccoli, cabbage; also produced by gut bacteria.
Deficiency: Bleeding disorders.

Water-Soluble Vitamins

These vitamins dissolve in water and are not stored in the body. Daily intake is necessary, as excess is excreted via urine. Includes Vitamin C and the B-complex group.

Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

Powerful antioxidant, boosts immunity, aids collagen formation, improves iron absorption.
Sources: Oranges, lemons, guava, grapes, strawberries, tomatoes, bell peppers, leafy vegetables.
Deficiency: Scurvy, bleeding gums, weakness.

B-Complex Vitamins

Support energy production, metabolism, nerve function, and red blood cell formation.

Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)

Sources: Whole grains, nuts, seeds, meat, legumes.
Deficiency: Beriberi.

Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)

Sources: Milk, eggs, leafy vegetables, almonds, yogurt.
Deficiency: Cracked lips, sore throat.

Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Sources: Meat, poultry, peanuts, fish, whole grains.
Deficiency: Pellagra.

Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid)

Sources: Eggs, meat, whole grains, vegetables.
Deficiency: Rare, may cause fatigue.

Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

Sources: Fish, bananas, potatoes, nuts, chicken.
Deficiency: Irritability, anemia.

Vitamin B7 (Biotin)

Sources: Eggs, nuts, seeds, sweet potatoes.
Deficiency: Hair loss, skin problems.

Vitamin B9 (Folate / Folic Acid)

Sources: Leafy greens, lentils, beans, citrus fruits.
Deficiency: Anemia, developmental issues.

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Sources: Meat, fish, eggs, dairy products.
Deficiency: Pernicious anemia, nerve damage.

Essential Minerals

Minerals are inorganic nutrients required for various body functions, including bone health, nerve signaling, muscle contraction, and enzyme activation. They are divided into macro-minerals and trace minerals based on the amounts needed by the body.

Calcium

Vital for strong bones and teeth, blood clotting, and muscle function.
Sources: Milk, cheese, yogurt, leafy greens, almonds.
Deficiency: Weak bones, osteoporosis, muscle cramps.

Iron

Essential for red blood cell production and oxygen transport.
Sources: Red meat, lentils, beans, spinach, fortified cereals.
Deficiency: Anemia, fatigue, weakness.

Magnesium

Supports muscle and nerve function, energy production, and bone health.
Sources: Nuts, seeds, whole grains, dark chocolate, leafy greens.
Deficiency: Muscle cramps, fatigue, irregular heartbeat.

Potassium

Regulates fluid balance, nerve signals, and muscle contractions.
Sources: Bananas, oranges, potatoes, spinach, tomatoes.
Deficiency: Weakness, cramps, irregular heartbeat.

Zinc

Supports immunity, wound healing, and DNA synthesis.
Sources: Meat, seafood, nuts, seeds, legumes.
Deficiency: Impaired immunity, hair loss, delayed wound healing.
